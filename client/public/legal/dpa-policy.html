Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

Effective Date: March 26, 2025

This DPA forms part of the CVitaePRO Terms of Service. This Data Processing Addendum (“DPA”) is entered into by and between:

One Guy Productions, LLC ("Processor", “we”, “us”), operator of the CVitaePRO platform,
and
You, the user or organization accessing the Service ("Controller").

This DPA governs the processing of Personal Data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (“GDPR”) and applies to the extent that the Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

1. Definitions

  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
  • Processing: Any operation performed on Personal Data, such as collection, storage, use, access, or erasure.
  • Data Controller: The party determining the purposes and means of the Processing.
  • Data Processor: The party processing Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
  • Sub-processor: A third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data.

2. Scope and Roles

  • The Controller instructs the Processor to process Personal Data as necessary to provide the CVitaePRO services.
  • The Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller unless required by law.

3. Categories of Personal Data

The Processor may process the following categories of Personal Data:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Username
  • Resume content (including education, experience, contact info)
  • IP address and browser metadata
  • Company name (for cover letter generation)

Special categories of data (sensitive data) are not knowingly collected and should not be submitted by the Controller.

4. Purpose of Processing

Personal Data is processed solely for the following purposes:

  • Providing and improving the CVitaePRO service
  • AI-enhanced resume and cover letter generation
  • Data storage and user access
  • Rate-limiting, fraud detection, and abuse prevention
  • Legal compliance

5. Obligations of the Processor

  • Process data only on Controller’s instructions
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect data
  • Ensure confidentiality of personnel with access to data
  • Assist the Controller in responding to user data rights (e.g., erasure, access)
  • Notify the Controller of any data breach without undue delay
  • Maintain a record of processing activities

6. Sub-processing

The Controller authorizes the Processor to engage sub-processors for specific tasks, such as:

  • OpenAI – for AI model usage
  • Cloud infrastructure providers (e.g., AWS, Render, etc.)
  • Database hosting providers

Processor will ensure all sub-processors are bound by written agreements with data protection obligations no less protective than this DPA. The Controller may request a list of current subprocessors at any time.

7. International Data Transfers

The Processor may transfer data to sub-processors located outside the EEA, provided:

  • Transfers are governed by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission; or
  • The recipient country ensures an adequate level of data protection under GDPR Article 45.

8. Rights of the Data Subject

The Processor will assist the Controller in responding to:

  • Data access requests
  • Rectification or deletion requests
  • Objections to processing
  • Requests for data portability

The Processor will not respond directly to such requests unless authorized by the Controller.

9. Security Measures

Processor implements the following minimum security measures:

  • TLS encryption in transit
  • Hashed credentials (bcrypt)
  • Strict access controls
  • Activity logging and alerting
  • Regular vulnerability scans and monitoring

A full description of technical and organizational security measures is available upon request.

10. Data Breach Notification

If the Processor becomes aware of a Personal Data Breach, it shall notify the Controller within 72 hours of discovery and provide details including:

  • Nature of the breach
  • Categories and volume of data affected
  • Actions taken to mitigate the breach
  • Contact information for follow-up

11. Return or Deletion of Data

Upon termination of the agreement, the Processor will:

  • Delete all Personal Data upon request (unless retention is required by law), or
  • Return the data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format

Data backups will be purged in accordance with standard retention cycles.

12. Audits

Upon reasonable written notice and no more than once annually, the Controller may request:

  • Summary audit reports
  • Confirmation of compliance with this DPA
  • (If required by law) A direct audit by Controller or an agreed third-party auditor

13. Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, United States, and the GDPR as applicable.

14. Duration

This DPA is effective for as long as the Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
